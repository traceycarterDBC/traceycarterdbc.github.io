Ruby Classes

Sunday, May 30, 2015


A Ruby class is a category of objects. The objects of a class are all members that have certain aspects in common but, specifically, the details are different. The best way to understand this is by comparing it to a category in the natural world. A class is akin to a species. For example, while we are all Homo sapiens sapiens, we are not all the same person. Oprah Winfrey and Mahatma Gandhi, both Homo sapiens sapiens, are extremely different from each other. They differ in gender, nationality, generation, and many other ways. However, Oprah Winfrey and Mahatma Gandhi are much more alike than either is to an animal of a different species.

Classes exist so that we can collect, execute methods on, and define instances within that class. Using our metaphor, the Homo sapiens sapiens class, a.k.a. humans, has to exist in order to think about them all at once. Having certain characteristics in common or being able to do certain kinds of things is a way to think about sharing common methods. For example, humans have human rights, whether they are Mahatma Gandhi or a person living alone in the mountains who is unknown by anybody else. All are people. All have the same rights. All can think on a higher order than, say, a turtle. For this reason, all homo sapiens sapiens can be said to share the same methods or the same basic abilities.
